Two noise sources at opposite ends of cosine

alias_only (28,748) · person/org sharing a generic alias cosine_only (61,626) · PO/ticket/SKU string templates

Look-alike records (~68%, right hump). POs, tickets, SKUs. Their names look nearly identical (PO-...318 vs PO-...227), so the embedding flags them as similar, but they are obviously different records. The judge is 99% sure they differ, so we are paying it to state the obvious.

Same-name people and orgs (~32%, left hump). Two different people (or orgs) that just share a common name or alias, so they get compared even though the names are not actually similar.

One pile sits at high similarity, the other at low, with a gap between. So there is no single similarity cutoff that catches both. Each needs its own fix.

Topics and decisions specifically

Topics are 43.0% of the "different" verdicts and decisions are 6.7%, so topic or decision alone is 49.7%, about half the burn. Adding projects and products (the same kind of record noise) brings it to 66.4%. Gardener only pairs entities of the same type, so these are pure record noise, not real entities.

What is removable

A deterministic "same shape, different number" rule removes 56.8% with no LLM call. Scoping dedup away from record-style types removes 66.4%. Together (exact union, fully safe) they remove 68.0%. The orthogonal remainder is an alias document-frequency filter on the alias path, which pushes the ceiling toward 99%.

A quality finding, not only cost

Of the org pairs marked "different," 408 (11.2%) are near-identical and 136 are near-duplicates by name similarity. So gardener may be under-merging organizations even while over-spending on the record noise. Person dedup, by contrast, is clean (0.8% near-identical); it is just over-invoked.

Recommendation

  1. Stand up a dashboard (type x admission path x cosine) and a small labeled gold set so every filter is falsifiable.
  2. Ship the deterministic ID rule plus entity-type scoping behind a flag (~68%, no LLM, near-zero false-positive risk). Validate on the gold set first.
  3. Add the alias document-frequency filter for the person/org remainder.
  4. Route the near-identical org pairs to review to recover the missed merges.
